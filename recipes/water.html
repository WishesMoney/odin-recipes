Water...like a philosopher

Ubiquitous throughout the universe as two parts hydrogen(H), one part oxygen(O), water(H₂O) is the be-all-end-all beverage for all your parched needs. It boasts a boiling point of 100 °C (212 °F), and a melting point of 0 °C (32 °F). There is the same amount of water on Earth as there was when the Earth was formed. The water you drink from your faucet could contain molecules that dinosaurs drank (Gross!). Nearly 97% of the world's water is salty and otherwise undrinkable. Another 2% is locked in ice caps and glaciers. That leaves just 1% for the rats, their agriculture, residential warrens, manufacturing plants, communities, and personal needs(Source).

Today, we learn how to drink that 1%...like a philosopher.

    • Because the water has to come from somewhere, id est, the source.

  • Ladle/Bucket

    • For retrieving the water from the well.

  • Cup/Goblet/Mug/Empty Soda Bottle

    • The more pretentious your receptacle of choice, the more you honor yourself.

  • Mouth

    • The linchpin of the entire process.

  • Thirst

    • The mind knows what the body wants. The mind quantifies needs through concepts. The mind influences the world through its body by direction of its needs. The mind is thirsty.

HOW TO PREPARE RIGHT NOW?

  1. Position at the Source

    2. Be it well, reservoir, faucet, or natural spring, position yourself shy of an arm's length from the source. Do not get wet.

  2. Have cup of choice at the ready.

    3. Within arm's reach, on a stable surface. Do not place in water.

  3. Implement at the Ready

    4. Bring the implement (bucket, ladle, etc.) over the source.

  4. Dip

    5. Allow the water to flow into the empty implement. Raise it from the water before full.

  5. Pour

    6. Pour water at 45° angle from implement into cup. Tilt cup at 45°. Water should contact cup halfway down inner, lowest wall.
